URL

A URL (Uniform Resource Locator) is the address of a specific resource on the internet. It provides a standardized way to locate and access web pages, images, videos, and other digital content.

URLs consist of several components, including the protocol (e.g., http, https), the domain name (e.g., www.example.com), and the path to the specific resource (e.g., /path/to/resource).

URLs are essential for navigating the web, allowing users to access information and services by simply entering the URL into a web browser. They play a crucial role in the structure of the internet and are fundamental to how we interact with online content.
